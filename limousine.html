Limousine Documentation

Luxury vehicles with extended wheelbases for premium passenger transport

What Defines a Limousine?

Limousines are characterized by their extended wheelbases and luxury amenities for passenger comfort. Key features include:

Characteristic Typical Specification
Wheelbase Extension 30-60 inches (76-152cm) beyond standard
Passenger Capacity 6-10 passengers (standard), up to 20 in stretch limos
Rear Compartment Height ≥48 inches (122cm)
Door Configuration 4-6 doors, often with rear suicide doors
Partition Options Fixed or retractable glass/acrylic
Luxury Features Premium leather, wood trim, mood lighting

Limousine Categories

  • Executive Sedans: Slightly extended luxury cars (Mercedes S-Class Pullman)
  • Traditional Stretch Limos: 120-140 inches extended (Lincoln Town Car)
  • Super Stretch: 160+ inches extended (10+ passengers)
  • Exotic Limos: High-end brands (Rolls-Royce Phantom Extended)
  • Novelty Limos: Hummer, SUV, or themed vehicles

Premium Limousine Features

  • Entertainment: Multiple LCD screens, premium audio
  • Climate Control: Dual-zone or quad-zone systems
  • Seating: Reclining seats with massage functions
  • Lighting: LED mood lighting with color options
  • Refreshments: Mini-bars, refrigerators, glassware
  • Connectivity: WiFi, charging ports, conference systems

Technical Specifications

  • Overall Length: 240-300 inches (6.1-7.6m)
  • Fuel Economy: 12-18 MPG city / 18-24 MPG highway
  • Turning Radius: 45-55 feet (13.7-16.8m)
  • Structural Reinforcement: Boxed frame extensions
  • Window Tint: Usually 5-15% VLT for privacy
  • Weight: 5,500-7,500 lbs (2,500-3,400kg)

Advantages of Limousines

  • Spacious, comfortable passenger accommodation
  • Premium luxury appointments
  • Professional chauffeur service
  • Ideal for special occasions
  • Enhanced privacy features
  • Status symbol and impressive appearance

Disadvantages of Limousines

  • Extremely expensive to purchase and maintain
  • Difficult to maneuver in urban areas
  • High operating costs (fuel, insurance)
  • Limited parking options
  • Not practical for daily personal use
  • Requires professional driver

Limousine Quick Facts

  • Origin of Name From Limousin region of France (shepherd's hood)
