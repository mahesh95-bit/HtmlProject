Coupe Documentation

Stylish two-door vehicles with sporty design and performance

What Defines a Coupe?

A coupe is characterized by its fixed roof, two-door configuration, and often sportier proportions. Key features include:

Characteristic Typical Specification
Roofline Height 50-55 inches (1.27-1.4m)
Door Opening Width ≥24 inches (61cm)
Front Headroom ≥37 inches (94cm)
Weight Distribution 48/52 to 55/45 F/R
Lateral Acceleration 0.85-1.05g (sport models)
Braking Distance 100-130 feet from 60mph

Detailed Dimension Requirements

  • Overall Length: 175-195 inches (4.4-5m)
  • Wheelbase: 104-112 inches (2.6-2.8m)
  • Width: 70-76 inches (1.78-1.93m)
  • Rear Seat Access: Minimum 20" door opening height
  • Trunk Opening: ≥40 inches wide (102cm)

Performance Characteristics

  • 0-60 mph: 3.5-7.5 seconds (varies by model)
  • Top Speed: 130-200+ mph
  • Engine Options: Typically 4-12 cylinders
  • Power Output: 200-700+ horsepower
  • Transmission: 6-10 speed manuals/automatics

Design Elements

  • Roof Slope: 25-35 degree angle common
  • Pillar Configuration: Often pillarless designs
  • Wheel Size: 18-22 inch diameter common
  • Aerodynamics: Coefficient of lift -0.5 to -1.5
  • Weight: 3,000-4,500 lbs (1,360-2,040kg)

Advantages of Coupes

  • Sportier appearance than sedans
  • Better handling dynamics
  • More powerful engine options often available
  • Exclusive, upscale feel
  • Lower drag coefficient than SUVs

Disadvantages of Coupes

  • Limited rear seat accessibility
  • Reduced headroom for taller passengers
  • Less practical for families
  • Higher insurance costs
  • More expensive than comparable sedans
