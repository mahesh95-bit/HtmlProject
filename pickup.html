Pickup Truck Documentation

Versatile workhorses with open cargo beds and powerful capabilities

What Defines a Pickup Truck?

Pickup trucks combine passenger accommodations with an open cargo bed. Key characteristics include:

Characteristic Typical Specification
Bed Length Options 5.5/6.5/8 feet (1.67/1.98/2.44m)
Payload Capacity 1,500-3,000 lbs (680-1,360kg)
Towing Capacity 5,000-13,000 lbs (2,270-5,900kg)
Cab Configurations Regular/Extended/Crew
Bed Width Between Wheel Wells 48-52 inches (122-132cm)
Ground Clearance 8-11 inches (20-28cm)

Pickup Truck Configurations

  • Compact/Midsize: 200-225 inches long, 4-6 cylinder engines
  • Full-Size: 225-250 inches long, V6/V8 engines
  • Heavy-Duty: 250-275 inches long, turbo-diesel options
  • Cab Types: Regular (2 doors), Extended (small rear doors), Crew (4 full doors)
  • Bed Styles: Short (5.5'), Standard (6.5'), Long (8')

Performance Capabilities

  • Payload Ratings: 1,200-4,000 lbs (540-1,814kg)
  • Fifth-Wheel Towing: 12,000-30,000 lbs (5,440-13,608kg)
  • Bed Load Capacity: 100-150 lbs/sq ft (488-732kg/m²)
  • Axle Ratios: 3.08 to 4.10:1
  • Fuel Economy: 15-22 MPG city / 19-26 MPG highway

Special Truck Features

  • Bed Liners: Spray-on or drop-in protection
  • Tie-Down Systems: Cleats, rails, or bed-mounted systems
  • Power Outlets: 110V/220V available in bed
  • Tailgate Options: Multi-position, step, or power operation
  • Camera Systems: Bed-view and trailer-view cameras

Advantages of Pickup Trucks

  • Unmatched utility and versatility
  • Strong towing and hauling capabilities
  • Available four-wheel drive systems
  • High ground clearance for rough terrain
  • Durable construction for heavy use
  • Strong resale value

Disadvantages of Pickup Trucks

  • Poor fuel economy compared to cars
  • Large size makes urban driving difficult
  • Higher purchase and operating costs
  • Rough ride quality when unloaded
  • Limited rear visibility

Pickup Truck Quick Facts

  • First Mass-Produced Pickup 1925 Ford Model T Runabout
  • Market Share (2023) 20% of all vehicles sold in US
  • Most Popular Segment Full-Size Crew Cab (Ford F-150, Chevy Silverado)
  • Average Price Range $30,000 - $80,000+
  • Resale Value Retains ~50% of value after 5 years
