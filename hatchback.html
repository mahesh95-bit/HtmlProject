Hatchback Documentation

Compact cars with versatile cargo space and urban practicality

What Defines a Hatchback?

A hatchback is characterized by its rear door that swings upward to provide access to a shared passenger and cargo area. Key features include:

Characteristic Typical Specification
Liftgate Opening Width ≥36 inches (91cm)
Rear Seat Fold Angle ≤15° from horizontal
Load Floor Height ≤28 inches (71cm)
Cargo Length (seats down) ≥70 inches (178cm)
Drag Coefficient 0.30-0.35 Cd
Turning Circle 32-36 feet (9.8-11m)

Detailed Dimension Requirements

To be classified as a hatchback, a vehicle must meet these dimensional criteria:

  • Overall Length: 150-175 inches (3.8-4.4m)
  • Wheelbase: 98-104 inches (2.5-2.6m)
  • Height: 57-61 inches (1.45-1.55m)
  • Cargo Volume: 15-25 cu ft (425-708L) seats up / 40-55 cu ft (1,132-1,557L) seats down
  • Rear Wiper Coverage: ≥80% of rear glass area

Standard Hatchback Features

  • Rear Door: Single-piece liftgate with integrated window
  • Seating: 60/40 split-folding rear seats standard
  • Visibility: Rear wiper and defroster standard
  • Storage: Underfloor compartments common
  • Access: Lower load height than SUVs (typically 24-28 inches)
  • Versatility: Cargo volume increases 120-150% with seats folded

Performance Characteristics

  • Fuel Economy: 28-35 MPG city / 36-44 MPG highway
  • Acceleration: 0-60 mph in 7-10 seconds (base models)
  • Handling: Shorter wheelbase improves maneuverability
  • Hot Hatches: Performance variants achieve 0-60 in 4-6 seconds
  • Weight: 2,600-3,200 lbs (1,180-1,450 kg)

Advantages of Hatchbacks

  • Excellent urban maneuverability with tight turning circles
  • Superior fuel economy compared to SUVs
  • Versatile cargo space with fold-flat seats
  • Lower purchase price and operating costs
  • Easier parking in tight spaces
  • Sportier handling than equivalent sedans

Disadvantages of Hatchbacks

  • Limited rear passenger headroom in some models
  • Less cargo capacity than SUVs or wagons
  • Perceived as less premium than sedans
  • Potentially noisier cargo area
  • Fewer luxury options in some markets
