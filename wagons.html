Wagon Documentation

Extended roofline vehicles combining sedan handling with SUV cargo space

What Defines a Wagon?

Wagons extend the roofline of sedans to create additional cargo space while maintaining car-like handling. Key characteristics:

Characteristic Typical Specification
Cargo Volume (seats up) 30-40 cu ft (850-1,132L)
Cargo Volume (seats down) 60-75 cu ft (1,700-2,124L)
Load Floor Height 24-28 inches (61-71cm)
Rear Liftgate Opening ≥45 inches wide (114cm)
Roof Rails Standard on most models
Weight Distribution 50/50 to 55/45 F/R

Wagon Dimensions

  • Length: 185-200 inches (4.7-5.1m)
  • Wheelbase: 108-117 inches (2.74-2.97m)
  • Height: 58-62 inches (1.47-1.57m)
  • Cargo Length (seats down): ≥75 inches (1.9m)
  • Rear Seat Fold: Flat or near-flat folding

Utility Features

  • Load Capacity: 1,000-1,500 lbs (454-680kg)
  • Towing Capacity: 1,500-3,500 lbs (680-1,588kg)
  • Roof Load Capacity: 150-200 lbs (68-91kg) dynamic
  • Hidden Storage: Underfloor compartments common
  • Tie-down Points: 4-8 standard in cargo area

Performance Characteristics

  • Fuel Economy: 22-28 MPG city / 30-36 MPG highway
  • 0-60 mph: 5.5-8.5 seconds (varies by engine)
  • Handling: Lower center of gravity than SUVs
  • Drag Coefficient: 0.28-0.32 Cd
  • Weight: 3,500-4,500 lbs (1,588-2,041kg)

Advantages of Wagons

  • Nearly SUV cargo capacity with better handling
  • Superior fuel economy compared to SUVs
  • Lower load floor than SUVs for easier loading
  • More aerodynamic than SUVs
  • Often available with performance variants

Disadvantages of Wagons

  • Limited ground clearance
  • Fewer model choices in North America
  • Perceived as less stylish than SUVs
  • Often more expensive than equivalent sedans
  • Reduced aftermarket support
