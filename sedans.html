Sedan Documentation

The classic four-door configuration offering comfort and practicality for families and commuters

What Defines a Sedan?

A sedan is a passenger car in a three-box configuration with separate compartments for engine, passenger, and cargo. The key characteristics that define a sedan include:

Characteristic Typical Specification
Number of doors 4 (sometimes 2 for coupe sedans)
Passenger capacity 4-5 adults
Roof height 54-60 inches (137-152 cm)
Length 175-200 inches (445-508 cm) for midsize
Wheelbase 105-115 inches (267-292 cm)
Trunk volume 12-16 cubic feet (340-453 liters)

Detailed Dimension Requirements

To be classified as a sedan, a vehicle must meet certain dimensional criteria:

  • Height-to-length ratio: Typically between 0.28 and 0.34
  • Ground clearance: Usually 5-6 inches (13-15 cm) for standard sedans
  • Trunk opening height: Minimum 18 inches (46 cm) for practical access
  • Rear seat legroom: At least 35 inches (89 cm) for comfortable seating
  • Shoulder room: Minimum 54 inches (137 cm) front, 52 inches (132 cm) rear

Most Common Sedan Features

  • Body Structure: Monocoque/unibody construction
  • Suspension: Independent front and rear suspension
  • Drivetrain: Front-wheel drive (common), available AWD/RWD
  • Safety: Standard airbags, ABS, stability control
  • Comfort: Center console, armrests, climate control
  • Technology: Touchscreen infotainment, Bluetooth
  • Storage: Center console storage, door pockets, glovebox

Average Fuel Mileage

Sedan fuel efficiency varies by size and drivetrain:

  • Compact sedans: 28-35 MPG city, 38-45 MPG highway
  • Midsize sedans: 24-30 MPG city, 33-40 MPG highway
  • Full-size sedans: 20-26 MPG city, 29-35 MPG highway
  • Luxury sedans: 18-24 MPG city, 26-32 MPG highway
  • Hybrid sedans: 40-55 MPG combined

Note: These are averages based on 2023 model year EPA estimates. Actual mileage may vary.

Advantages of Sedans

  • Better fuel efficiency than SUVs/trucks
  • Lower purchase price than comparable SUVs
  • Easier to park and maneuver in cities
  • Smoother ride quality and handling
  • Lower center of gravity improves safety
  • Better aerodynamics reduces wind noise
  • Typically lower maintenance costs

Disadvantages of Sedans

  • Less cargo space than SUVs/wagons
  • Lower ground clearance limits off-road capability
  • More difficult entry/exit for some passengers
  • Reduced visibility compared to taller vehicles
  • Less towing capacity than larger vehicles
  • Perceived as less "prestigious" than SUVs in some markets

Quick Facts

  • First Production Sedan 1915 Studebaker (first closed-body car)
  • Market Share (2023) 22% of all passenger vehicles sold
  • Most Popular Segment Midsize sedans (Toyota Camry, Honda Accord)
  • Average Price $25,000 (compact) to $40,000 (midsize)
  • Resale Value Retains ~45% of value after 5 years
