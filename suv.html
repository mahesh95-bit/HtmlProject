SUV Documentation

Sport Utility Vehicles combining passenger space with rugged capability

What Defines an SUV?

SUVs (Sport Utility Vehicles) are characterized by their tall build, elevated ground clearance, and available all-wheel drive systems. Key characteristics include:

Characteristic Typical Specification
Ground clearance 6.5-11 inches (16.5-28 cm)
Approach/departure angles 18°-30° approach, 20°-25° departure
Seating capacity 5-8 passengers
Cargo volume 30-100+ cubic feet (850-2830 liters)
Weight 3,500-6,000 lbs (1,600-2,700 kg)
Towing capacity 1,500-9,000 lbs (680-4,080 kg)

SUV Size Categories

  • Subcompact/Crossover: Under 175 inches long (Honda HR-V, Mazda CX-30)
  • Compact: 175-185 inches (Toyota RAV4, Ford Escape)
  • Midsize: 185-200 inches (Toyota Highlander, Ford Explorer)
  • Full-size: Over 200 inches (Chevrolet Suburban, Ford Expedition)
  • Luxury: Various sizes with premium features (Range Rover, BMW X7)

Standard SUV Features

  • Drivetrain: Available AWD/4WD systems
  • Suspension: Independent or solid rear axle
  • Safety: Electronic stability control, rollover mitigation
  • Utility: Roof rails, folding seats, trailer hitch
  • Comfort: Higher seating position, command view
  • Technology: Off-road modes, terrain management

Performance Capabilities

  • Fuel Economy: 19-28 MPG city, 23-32 MPG highway
  • Water Fording: 20-35 inches (50-90 cm) for off-road models
  • Payload Capacity: 1,200-2,500 lbs (540-1,130 kg)
  • Hill Descent Control: Standard on many models
  • Skid Plates: Available on off-road trims

SUV Advantages

  • Higher seating position improves visibility
  • Greater cargo capacity than sedans
  • Available all-wheel/4-wheel drive
  • Better capability in poor weather
  • Higher ground clearance for rough roads
  • Versatile interior configurations
  • Strong towing capacity on larger models

SUV Disadvantages

  • Lower fuel efficiency than cars
  • Higher purchase and operating costs
  • More difficult to park in urban areas
  • Higher center of gravity affects handling
  • Larger blind spots than passenger cars
  • More expensive tires and maintenance

SUV Quick Facts

  • First Modern SUV 1984 Jeep Cherokee (XJ)
  • Market Share (2023) 45% of all vehicles sold in US
  • Most Popular Segment Compact SUVs (Toyota RAV4, Honda CR-V)
  • Average Price Range $28,000 (compact) to $80,000 (luxury)
  • Off-road Capability Varies from pavement-only to rock-crawling
